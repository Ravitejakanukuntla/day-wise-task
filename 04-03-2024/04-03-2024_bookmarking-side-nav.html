Chapter-1

Chicken Biryani

  Ingredients:

  
For marinating the chicken:

  - 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  - 1 cup yogurt
  - 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  - 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  - 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  - 1 teaspoon garam masala
  - Salt to taste
  - Juice of 1 lemon

  
  

For the rice:

- 2 cups basmati rice, soaked in water for 30 minutes and drained
- 4 cups water
- 2 bay leaves
- 4 green cardamom pods
- 4 cloves
- 1 cinnamon stick
- Salt to taste


For cooking the biryani:

- 4 tablespoons ghee or oil
- 2 large onions, thinly sliced
- 2 tomatoes, chopped
- 2 green chilies, slit
- 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
- 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves
- Saffron strands soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk
- Fried onions (optional)
- Cashews and raisins for garnish (optional)


Instructions:


1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice.
 Mix well to coat the chicken evenly with the marinade. Cover and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.

2. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add soaked and drained basmati rice along with bay leaves, cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, and salt. Cook the 
rice until it's 70-80% done. Drain the excess water and set the rice aside.

3. In a separate large pot or deep skillet, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add thinly sliced onions and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove
 half of the fried onions and set aside for layering.

4. To the remaining onions in the pot, add chopped tomatoes and slit green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and oil starts to separate.

5. Add marinated chicken pieces to the pot along with chopped mint leaves and coriander leaves. Mix well and cook for about 10-15 minutes until the chicken is 
partially cooked and the gravy thickens.

6. In a separate small bowl, soak saffron strands in warm milk and set aside.

7. Once the chicken is partially cooked, layer the partially cooked rice evenly over the chicken in the pot.

8. Sprinkle the reserved fried onions, soaked saffron milk, cashews, and raisins (if using) on top of the rice layer.

9. Cover the pot tightly with a lid or aluminum foil to seal in the flavors. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes, allowing the biryani to steam and the flavors to
 meld together.

10. Once done, remove the pot from the heat and let it rest for 5-10 minutes before opening the lid.

11. Gently mix the biryani from the sides, fluffing up the rice and mixing it with the chicken and spices.

12. Serve the chicken biryani hot with raita (yogurt sauce), salad, or your favorite chutney.

Enjoy your delicious homemade chicken biryani!

Chapter-3

Chicken Rolls

  Ingredients:

  
For the chicken filling:

  - 500 grams boneless chicken breast, cut into small pieces
  - 1 onion, finely chopped
  - 2 cloves garlic, minced
  - 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  - 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  - 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  - 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  - 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  - Salt to taste
  - 2 tablespoons oil

  - Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (optional)
- Juice of 1/2 lemon


For the rolls:

- Ready-made tortillas or chapatis
- Lettuce leaves
- Sliced cucumber
- Sliced tomatoes
- Sliced onions
- Mayonnaise or your favorite sauce
- Optional: grated cheese, sliced avocado, pickles, or any other desired fillings


Instructions:


1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.

2. Add minced garlic and ginger paste to the pan. Sauté for another minute until fragrant.

3. Add the chicken pieces to the pan and cook until they are no longer pink and start to brown.

4. Stir in the red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well to coat the chicken pieces with the spices. Cook for a few
 more minutes until the chicken is cooked through and tender.

5. Once the chicken is cooked, squeeze the lemon juice over it and add chopped fresh coriander leaves if desired. Mix well and remove from heat. Let the chicken 
filling cool slightly.

6. Warm the tortillas or chapatis on a skillet or in the microwave for a few seconds to make them pliable.

7. To assemble the rolls, place a lettuce leaf on each tortilla or chapati. Spoon some of the chicken filling onto the lettuce leaf.

8. Add sliced cucumber, tomatoes, onions, and any other desired fillings on top of the chicken filling.

9. Drizzle mayonnaise or your favorite sauce over the fillings.

10. Roll up the tortilla or chapati tightly, tucking in the sides as you go to form a neat roll.

11. Secure the rolls with toothpicks if needed.

12. Serve the chicken rolls immediately, either whole or sliced into smaller pieces for appetizers or snacks.

Enjoy your delicious Chicken Rolls!
